When stories become alive

Home | All Products

I've always had a thing for hand crafts and painting. Ever since we were about 7 years old, me and my sister used to build furniture and clothes for our dolls, make friendship braceletes, Peruvian earrings, cardboard masks, origami and whatever else we came across of back then.

Neverthless, the story of this project starts much later, somewhere around September last year when I wanted to find a special gift for a friend's child. I came across this lovely hand-puppet made put of wool(a baby orangutan) and I instantly fell in love with it. I loved the fact that it was made out of natural fabric and that the moment after you place it in your hand it becomes alive and can tell stories. After the package arrived and I got to play around with it I just had to know how it was made... and that was it!

Since then I have learned to wet- and needle-felt toys, accessories and wool paintings, mostly because I wanted to explore the incredible versatility of this material, but also in order to find out what I like the most.
